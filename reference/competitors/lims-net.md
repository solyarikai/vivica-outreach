## Market position

**Locasoft** product, French-origin, EU-focused. Limited US adoption. Shows up in NJ labs that have European tech preferences or French-speaking ownership. The internal team flagged it because some russian-segment labs in NJ use it (per Petr's chat).

Sales motion: regional, EU primary, US secondary. No major US presence.

## Where Vivica wins

Almost everywhere when the customer is in the US clinical market:

- **US compliance native** (CLIA / CAP / HIPAA)
- **US support hours** and US-based team
- **English-first product** (some lims.net interfaces still feel translated rather than localized)
- **Modern cloud-native** vs Locasoft's older deployment patterns
- **Implementation speed** for US clinical workflows

## Where lims.net wins

- **EU compliance and regional features** if a customer operates in France or EU
- **Existing customer comfort** if they're already deeply embedded
- **Lower price point** in some regions

## Migration angle

Three-phase framework applies. Migrations from lims.net to Vivica are often driven by **growth past the platform's US ceiling** — the lab outgrows what lims.net does in the US market.

Translation overhead: lims.net interfaces, documentation, and exports may have French-language artifacts. Mirror phase needs a translation pass on configuration documents.
