## Market position

LabWare is the global LIMS market leader. Strongest in **pharmaceutical and biotech R&D** — NIH, GSK, Pfizer types. Their clinical-lab footprint is real but smaller and skews to large reference labs and hospital systems.

Sales motion: enterprise field sales, six-month-plus sales cycles, six-figure annual contracts, mandatory professional services. Not a self-serve product. The brand is "safe, established, customizable to anything."

## Where Vivica wins

- **Implementation timeline**: LabWare implementations frequently run 12-24 months. Vivica targets 4-6 weeks.
- **TCO**: LabWare's cost is dominated by professional services and customization. Vivica's per-sample cloud pricing is predictable.
- **Modernization**: LabWare's UX is built on a long-running enterprise stack. Cloud-native UX feels markedly different to end users.
- **Vendor lock-in**: Every LabWare customization is a billable change order. Vivica's configuration-first model means most changes don't need vendor involvement.

## Where LabWare wins

Be honest where they're stronger — losing on these is fine, we're not their target:

- **Pharma/biotech R&D workflows** with heavy stability/regulatory documentation — LabWare's home turf
- **Highly customized installations** at large sites that have invested years in their LabWare config
- **Regulatory paper trail** for FDA-regulated manufacturing — they have the longest track record
- **Enterprise procurement** — they fit comfortably into a Big-IT decision process

## Who's a good Vivica target moving from LabWare

Per the ICP cross-reference:

- **Mid-size clinical or reference labs** that bought LabWare 5-10 years ago and have hit the cost-of-customization wall
- **Labs whose Lab IT lead just left** (the only person who knew the customizations)
- **Multi-site operations** consolidating onto one platform (LabWare's per-site licensing makes this expensive)
- **Recently acquired labs** under PE ownership pushing for tech consolidation

## Migration angle

Universal three-phase framework applies (`../playbooks/three-phase-migration.md`):

- **Mirror phase**: configure Vivica to read LabWare's data exports (HL7, ASTM, custom CSV — all common in LabWare exports). Recreate test catalogs, clients, instrument profiles.
- **Proxy phase**: dual-run for 30+ days. LabWare keeps producing official results; Vivica produces parallel results. Compare daily.
- **Decommission phase**: cutover by service line, not all at once. Clinical chem first, then AP/molecular last.

Specific to LabWare: their ETL exports are well-documented. We typically use their standard `Run Result Export` format as the migration source.
