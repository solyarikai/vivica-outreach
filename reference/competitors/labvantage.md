## Market position

Owned by **The Chatterjee Group**. Originally sold as a more configurable alternative to LabWare in the pharma space. About one-third of public reviews come from pharma users; the rest spread across CRO, clinical, and food/environmental.

Sales motion: enterprise sales, multi-month evaluations, configuration-heavy deployments.

## Where Vivica wins

- **Reporting customization**: LabVantage's reporting is notorious for requiring Crystal Reports / Business Objects expertise. Vivica's drag-drop builder doesn't need a BI specialist.
- **Cloud-native deployment**: LabVantage offers hosted, but it's lift-and-shift, not native multi-tenant SaaS.
- **Total cost of ownership**: similar pattern to LabWare — base license is just the start, configuration adds significant cost.

## Where LabVantage wins

- **Configurability for niche pharma workflows** (stability studies, dissolution, formulation development)
- **Established install base** with documented validation packs

## Migration angle

Standard three-phase framework. LabVantage exports are typically structured (the platform's biggest strength is its data model), so mirror phase is technically smooth. Pain is usually around **report recreation** — labs have spent years building Crystal/BO reports they need to bring forward.
