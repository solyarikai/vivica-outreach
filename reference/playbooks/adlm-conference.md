# Playbook — ADLM 2026 Conference

The single biggest event of this project's calendar year. Annual meeting of AACC (Association for Diagnostics & Laboratory Medicine, formerly American Association for Clinical Chemistry).

> **Conference fact sheet**:
> - **Dates**: 26-30 July 2026
> - **Location**: Anaheim Convention Center, CA
> - **Attendance**: ~15,000 attendees, 850+ exhibitors
> - **Days from project start (10 May 2026)**: ~11 weeks until conference start

This playbook covers three distinct outreach phases: **pre-conference**, **onsite**, **post-conference**. Each has different cadence, tone, and tooling.

## Why ADLM matters

- **Concentration**: 11 weeks of normal outreach won't surface as many decision-makers as 5 days at ADLM
- **Buying intent**: people register and pay to attend specifically because they're shopping
- **Booth presence**: face-to-face beats every cold email metric — if we get a meeting on the show floor, conversion rates flip from 1-2% reply to 30-50% qualified meeting
- **Vivica visibility**: even if Vivica doesn't have a booth, the team can attend and meet pre-booked prospects

The campaigns built around ADLM are the highest-ROI campaigns of the year. Don't dilute them with parallel general-purpose campaigns during the same window.

## Three phases

```
Pre-conference  (4-6 weeks before, ~weeks of June 1 — July 19)
   │
   │  Goal: book on-floor meetings BEFORE the show
   ▼
Onsite          (July 26-30)
   │
   │  Goal: real-time follow-up; same-day responses; net-new contacts from booth
   ▼
Post-conference (1-3 weeks after, July 31 — August 21)
   │
   │  Goal: convert the "we talked at ADLM" connections into pipeline
   ▼
[transition back to evergreen segment campaigns]
```

## Phase 1 — Pre-conference (June 1 — July 19)

**Goal**: book as many on-floor meetings as possible before the conference starts.

### Inputs needed (from Chris/Andrew)

- ADLM 2026 attendees list (purchased from AACC or scraped from public registration data)
- Confirmation: does Vivica have a booth? If yes, what's the booth number and hours?
- Vivica team availability during conference hours (which days, which staffers)
- Any pre-scheduled events (pre-conference workshop, sponsored session, hospitality dinner)

### Sequence pattern

Pre-conference outreach has a **stronger CTA** than evergreen sequences because there's a real, dated incentive:

```
Email 1 (T-6 weeks):
  Subject: catching up at ADLM in Anaheim?
  Hook: "saw you registered for ADLM" (if attendee list confirms)
  Pain: persona-specific (one of: TAT / audit / migration / cost)
  Pivot: "Vivica team will be on the floor July 26-30"
  CTA: "Want to grab 20 min at the booth (#XXXX) — Tuesday or Wednesday?"

Email 2 (T-4 weeks):
  Subject: 20 min at booth #XXXX?
  Hook: reference to Email 1 + Acure case study angle
  CTA: explicit calendar slots, specific times

Email 3 (T-2 weeks):
  Subject: ADLM next week — still room
  Hook: urgency + "filling slots fast"
  CTA: shorter, "10-min coffee at booth?"

Email 4 (T-3 days):
  Subject: see you at ADLM?
  Hook: confirmation, even if no prior reply
  CTA: "stop by booth #XXXX anytime — I'll keep an open slot"
```

Spacing reflects ADLM-specific cadence (closer to event = more frequent), not standard outreach pattern.

### What goes in the subject line

- Mention "ADLM" by name in every email — anchors urgency
- Mention "Anaheim" in some — locality cue
- Avoid clickbait — these are professionals who hate hyperbole

### What kills pre-conference

- ❌ Sending pre-conference emails to people not attending — annoying, wastes the moment
- ❌ Pretending Vivica has a booth when it doesn't (if no booth, pivot to "I'll be on the floor — coffee?")
- ❌ Vague meeting times — give explicit slots
- ❌ Generic attendee approach — segment by persona × current LIMS just like normal sequences

### Calendar mechanics

For meetings that book, use a tool the prospect can self-serve into (Calendly link or similar). Avoid email back-and-forth on scheduling — the conference window is too short.

## Phase 2 — Onsite (July 26-30)

**Goal**: real-time follow-up; same-day responses; capture new contacts from booth interactions.

### Operating rhythm

- **Morning (7-9am Pacific)**: review previous day's booth contacts, send "thanks for stopping by" within 12 hours of meeting
- **Daytime (booth hours)**: face-to-face interactions, business-card collection, quick demos
- **Evening (8-10pm Pacific)**: triage day's contacts, queue next-day follow-ups, post on LinkedIn about what's working at the conference

### Sequence pattern (onsite)

**Same-day "thanks" within 12 hours of meeting**:

```
Subject: great talking at ADLM today
Hook: 1-2 specific things from the conversation (their lab name, the pain they mentioned)
Body: short — 3 sentences
CTA: send the specific resource you promised on the floor
  (sample audit-trail export, TCO model template, Acure reference details, etc.)
```

Speed matters more than polish. A 12-hour follow-up that's slightly imperfect beats a 72-hour follow-up that's perfectly written.

### What goes in onsite emails

- The prospect's lab name — they met dozens of vendors, you need to anchor "we talked"
- One specific thing they said — proves you listened
- One follow-up artifact — sample export, Acure reference detail, TCO model

### What kills onsite

- ❌ Generic "thanks for stopping by" with no specifics — they've gotten 50 of these
- ❌ Long emails — they're tired, hot, on day 3 of conference
- ❌ Same-day demo asks — they're not in their office; ask for "next week" instead

## Phase 3 — Post-conference (July 31 — August 21)

**Goal**: convert connections into pipeline.

### Three weeks of structured follow-up

```
Week 1 (Jul 31 — Aug 6):
  Sequence to all booth contacts who haven't replied
  Sequence to all pre-conference contacts who didn't book a meeting
  Subject: "follow-up from ADLM" anchor

Week 2 (Aug 7 — Aug 13):
  Sequence to LinkedIn-connections-made-at-ADLM
  Subject: "since we connected at ADLM..."

Week 3 (Aug 14 — Aug 21):
  Final ADLM-themed touch
  Subject: "before ADLM context fades..."

After Week 3:
  Drop ADLM framing entirely. Roll prospects back into evergreen sequences
  by segment (CLIA-fresh, using-LabWare, etc.) — see other plans.
```

### What goes in post-conference emails

- Anchor in the conversation or the conference moment (not "as a leading clinical-LIMS provider...")
- Reference any specific request from the conversation — "you mentioned wanting to see how AP narrative reports look — here's the demo"
- Vivica team is now back at base; offer normal demo / call options without conference urgency
- Acure case study works again here — strong reference

### What kills post-conference

- ❌ Pretending you remember conversations you didn't have
- ❌ Continuing ADLM urgency past Week 3 — feels desperate
- ❌ Switching to generic sequences too fast (within Week 1) — wastes the warm context

## ICP overlap with conference attendees

Not every ADLM attendee fits Vivica's ICP. Pre-conference, filter the attendee list against:

- Hospital labs → out (Epic/Cerner lock-in)
- Pharma R&D → out (LabWare's home turf, not our market)
- POL / PSC / Reference Lab → in
- Vendor employees → out (peers, not prospects)

Once filtered, segment by persona × current LIMS just like normal sequences.

- **Evgenia**: optional booth support

## What we publish before/during/after

For Vivica's own LinkedIn / website (Chris coordinates):

- **Pre-conference (T-2 weeks)**: announcement post — "see you at ADLM, booth #XXXX"
- **Day of conference (each day)**: short post about what's interesting, who we met
- **Post-conference (Week 1)**: recap post — themes we heard, what's next

This gives ammunition for the email sequences ("just published our ADLM recap — covers the [specific topic] you mentioned...") and builds visibility for non-attendees.
