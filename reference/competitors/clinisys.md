## Market position

Clinisys is the **incumbent** for many clinical labs. The product family includes legacy brands like **Sunquest**, **Horizon**, and others that were rolled up over time. They're pushing cloud modernization, but the install base sits on aging on-prem deployments and the modernization story is multi-year.

Sales motion: enterprise field sales, replacement cycles measured in years, deep integration into hospital reference workflows.

## Where Vivica wins

- **Modernization without rip-and-replace**: three-phase mirror → proxy → decommission means a lab can move off Sunquest/Horizon incrementally instead of waiting for Clinisys's roadmap
- **Cloud-native UX**: Sunquest/Horizon UX is decades old; modern UX is a meaningful advantage
- **Vendor lock-in escape**: many Clinisys customers feel trapped by accumulated customizations
- **Pace of change**: cloud-native vendor ships features faster than enterprise multi-product roadmap

## Where Clinisys wins

- **Established hospital-system integrations** with Epic/Cerner — not Vivica's market anyway
- **Public health lab depth** — reportable disease registries, surveillance integrations
- **"Safe enterprise" procurement** — easy choice for risk-averse buyers
- **Large-scale reference labs** with multi-million-test annual volume already running on Clinisys

## Migration angle

Three-phase framework applies, but Clinisys migrations have an extra wrinkle: **legacy product line consolidation**. Customer might be on Sunquest, Horizon, or another acquired brand. Mirror phase needs explicit attention to which legacy product's data model is being mirrored.

Standard ETL exports exist (Sunquest in particular has well-documented HL7 / ASTM exports). Pain is usually around **historical data continuity** — many Clinisys customers have 10-20 years of result history that needs to be queryable post-migration.
