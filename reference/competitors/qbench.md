## Market position

QBench is the strongest **cloud-native LIMS competitor by reputation**. Modern UX, transparent pricing, multi-vertical. Their breadth (cannabis, food, environmental, clinical) is both strength and weakness — they serve many markets, but no single market gets purpose-built workflows.

Sales motion: self-serve trial → mid-market sales. Less enterprise-grade procurement than LabWare/LabVantage.

## Where Vivica wins

- **Clinical-specific workflows**: anatomic pathology, complex molecular reporting, narrative interpretation — QBench is broader and less deep here.
- **HIPAA / clinical-grade compliance posture**: QBench has clinical customers but their compliance surface is shaped for the multi-vertical case.
- **Reference-lab routing**: complex specimen routing between labs is a clinical-specialty pattern QBench addresses generically.

## Where QBench wins

- **Modern UX and onboarding speed** for non-clinical labs
- **Self-serve evaluation** — lab can sign up and try without sales contact
- **Multi-vertical operations** that genuinely span cannabis + food + clinical

## Migration angle

Three-phase framework applies. QBench exports are JSON-friendly and well-documented (their API is one of their selling points), so mirror phase is technically clean. Pain is usually **clinical features QBench doesn't have** — Vivica adds capability rather than replacing existing capability.
